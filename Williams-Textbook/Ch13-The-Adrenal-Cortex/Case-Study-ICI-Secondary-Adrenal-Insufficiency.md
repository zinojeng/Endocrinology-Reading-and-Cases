# Case Study: ICI-Induced Secondary Adrenal Insufficiency & Critical Illness Steroid Management

> **Source Reference**: Williams Textbook of Endocrinology 14th Ed. Chapter 13 (The Adrenal Cortex) & Chapter 48 (Endocrine Disorders of Critical Illness)
> **Clinical Case**: Metastatic Head & Neck SCC (cT0N3bM1) s/p Pembrolizumab (Keytruda) C26 presenting with severe hypocortisolemia.

---

## 📋 Clinical Case Overview

### Patient History & Problem List
- **Primary Diagnosis**: Metastatic Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC), primary unknown (favored head & neck), cT0N3bM1, Stage IVC.
- **Immunotherapy**: Pembrolizumab (Keytruda, anti-PD-1) C26 (2024/12/17 – 2026/07/15).
- **Prior Steroids**: Prednisolone (2024/12/31 – 2025/05/06) for ICI-induced skin rash (discontinued ~14 months ago).
- **Comorbidities**: Malignant cachexia, Acute Kidney Injury (AKI, dehydration vs. Cisplatin nephropathy), multiple gastric/duodenal ulcers, hypomagnesemia.
- **Acute Medication Event**: Administered **Dexamethasone 10 mg stat** on 2026/07/22.

## 🎯 Fellow High-Yield Learning Points

> 📌 **重點 (High-Yield)**：
> 1. **Serum Cortisol < 1.0 µg/dL + ACTH 6.3 pg/mL** confirms <u>Central (Secondary) Adrenal Insufficiency</u>. In primary AI, ACTH would be markedly elevated (> 100–1000 pg/mL).
> 2. **Anti-PD-1 (Pembrolizumab) Endocrinopathy**: Classic presentation is **isolated ACTH deficiency** with a preserved thyroid and gonadal axis, unlike anti-CTLA-4 agents which typically cause pan-hypophysitis.
> 3. **Dexamethasone 10 mg stat Pharmacology**: Dexamethasone does <u>NOT cross-react with cortisol immunoassays</u>, making it the emergency steroid of choice prior to/during diagnostic ACTH stimulation testing. However, 10 mg is a high pharmacological dose (~266 mg hydrocortisone equivalent).

## 🔍 Differential Diagnosis & Clinical Reasoning

```
  │ - Hyperkalemia (Aldo ↓) │                         │ - Normal K+ (Aldo intact)   │

  └─────────────────────────┘                         └─────────────────────────────┘
                                                                     │
                                                      ┌──────────────┴──────────────┐
                                                      ▼                             ▼
                                           ICI-Induced ACTH Def.          Prior Steroid Suppression
                                           (Pembrolizumab C26)            (Prednisolone stopped > 1 yr)
```

- **Pathophysiology (Williams Ch 13 §2)**:
  - When serum cortisol drops below 3.0 µg/dL, the uninhibited hypothalamic-pituitary axis should secrete high amounts of ACTH.
  - An ACTH level of **6.3 pg/mL** in the setting of cortisol **< 1.0 µg/dL** represents loss of normal feedback response, pinpointing the lesion to the **pituitary/hypothalamus**.
  - **Aldosterone Preservation**: Mineralocorticoid production (zona glomerulosa) is regulated primarily by RAAS (renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system) and serum potassium, NOT ACTH. Therefore, secondary AI patients usually maintain normal potassium and do not require fludrocortisone replacement.

---

### 2. Etiology Analysis: ICI-Induced Isolated ACTH Deficiency vs. Exogenous Steroid Suppression

- **ICI (Pembrolizumab / Keytruda) Toxicity**:
  - Anti-PD-1 antibody treatment causes **isolated ACTH deficiency** in ~1% of patients.
  - Onset can be **late-emerging**, even after > 1-2 years of treatment (Cycle 26!).
  - **Preserved Thyroid Axis**: Note that TSH (2.520) and Free T4 (1.06) are completely normal. This dissociation (isolated corticotrope failure with normal thyrotropes) is characteristic of anti-PD-1 hypophysitis.
- **Prior Steroid Exposure**:
  - The patient received Prednisolone from Dec 2024 to May 2025 (~4 months).
  - HPA axis recovery following 4 months of daily steroids generally occurs within 6 to 12 months post-discontinuation. Because 14 months have passed, chronic steroid withdrawal alone is less likely the primary cause, though it may contribute to reduced HPA reserve.
- **Critical Illness-Related Corticosteroid Insufficiency (CIRCI) (Williams Ch 48 §3)**:
  - In critical illness, malignant cachexia, and sepsis, severe stress increases baseline cortisol demand.
  - A baseline cortisol < 10 µg/dL in a critically ill/stressed patient suggests CIRCI. A level < 1.0 µg/dL represents severe, life-threatening adrenocortical failure.

### 3. Pharmacology & Pitfalls of Dexamethasone 10 mg Stat

> 💡 **記憶法：DEX 救急三特點 (D-E-X)**
> - **D**iagnostic preservation: 不干擾 Cortisol Immunoassay 檢測。
> - **E**quivalent high potency: 10 mg Dex = ~266 mg Hydrocortisone (極高劑量抗發炎/降腦壓/抗過敏劑量)。
> - **X**-out HPA axis: 高劑量快速強效負回饋抑制 HPA 軸。

#### Key Pharmacologic Comparison Table

| Glucocorticoid | Equivalent Dose | Glucocorticoid Potency | Mineralocorticoid Potency | Cross-Reacts with Cortisol Assay? | Half-Life ($t_{1/2}$) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hydrocortisone** | 20 mg | 1 | 1 | <u>YES (Falsely elevates cortisol)</u> | 8–12 hrs |
| **Prednisolone** | 5 mg | 4 | 0.8 | <u>YES (Partial cross-reactivity)</u> | 18–36 hrs |
| **Dexamethasone** | **0.75 mg** | **30** | **0** | <u>NO (Zero immunoassay interference)</u> | **36–54 hrs** |

### 📊 表格解讀與臨床教訓 (Clinical Annotations for Fellows)

1. **Why Dexamethasone was chosen on 7/22**:
   - If an emergency ACTH stimulation test (Cosyntropin 250 µg IV) is planned, **Dexamethasone is the only steroid that can be administered without interfering with serum cortisol immunoassays**.
   - Giving Hydrocortisone prior to drawing blood will cause a false elevation of serum cortisol, masking adrenal insufficiency.
2. **Dose Considerations (10 mg Stat)**:
   - **Physiologic Maintenance**: Hydrocortisone 15–20 mg/day (equivalent to Dexamethasone 0.5–0.75 mg/day).
   - **Adrenal Crisis Dosing**: Hydrocortisone 100 mg IV stat, then 50 mg q6h (or Dexamethasone 4 mg IV stat).
   - **10 mg Dexamethasone**: Represents ~266 mg Hydrocortisone. In oncologic settings, 10 mg Dex is typically used for increased intracranial pressure / brain metastasis edema / severe ICI toxicity / spinal cord compression.
   - *Fellow Pearl*: Since baseline Cortisol (< 1.0 µg/dL) and ACTH (6.3 pg/mL) were already drawn on 7/15 (prior to 7/22 Dex), Central AI was **already diagnostically confirmed**. The stat Dex dose effectively treats acute glucocorticoid deficiency.

## 📝 High-Yield Exam & Board Review Summary

> 💡 **Williams Textbook Summary Pearls**:
> 1. **Anti-PD-1 (Pembrolizumab / Nivolumab)** → High risk for **Isolated ACTH Deficiency**. Pituitary MRI may be completely normal.
> 2. **Anti-CTLA-4 (Ipilimumab)** → High risk for **Pan-Hypophysitis** (Pituitary enlargement on MRI + multi-axis hormone deficits + headache).
> 3. **Central AI Diagnosis**: Cortisol < 3.0 µg/dL with non-elevated ACTH (< 20 pg/mL). Cosyntropin test peak cortisol < 18 µg/dL (or < 14–15 µg/dL on modern high-specificity assays).
> 4. **Steroid Choice during ACTH Test**: <u>Dexamethasone</u> is the ONLY steroid that does not cross-react with cortisol immunoassay.
