## Key Points / 重點摘要

**EN:**
- The use of continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) is transformative for diabetes management, improving glycemia, reducing acute complications, and improving quality of life.
- All individuals with diabetes, regardless of the diabetes type, therapy modality, or stage of the disease, benefit from the use of CGM.
- The use of CGM-derived metrics such as time in range (TIR), time below range (TBR), and time above range (TAR) facilitates day-to-day diabetes management, glycemic outcomes, and quality of life.
- Artificial intelligence decision-support systems (advisers) can facilitate the day-to-day use of CGM.
- Connected insulin pens (smart pens) can further facilitate treatment with multiple daily injections and improve glycemic outcomes.
- Insulin delivery with continuous subcutaneous insulin infusion (CSII, insulin pump) improves glycemia, reduces acute complications, and improves quality of life.
- Advanced hybrid closed-loop systems for automated insulin delivery (AID) currently represent the most successful treatment modality for individuals treated with insulin.
- Special considerations are important for different user populations, age groups, sick-day management, regular sport activities, and in-hospital and intensive care treatment.
- Better access to diabetes technologies for all individuals with diabetes is paramount.
- Further technologic developments may improve user-friendliness, additionally increase automation, and improve long-term user experience and satisfaction.

**中文：**
- 連續血糖監測（continuous glucose monitoring, CGM）對糖尿病管理具變革性，可改善血糖、減少急性併發症並提升生活品質。
- 無論糖尿病型別、治療方式或疾病階段，所有糖尿病患者皆可從 CGM 受益。
- 使用 CGM 衍生指標——time in range（TIR）、time below range（TBR）、time above range（TAR）——有助於每日糖尿病管理、血糖結果與生活品質。
- 人工智慧決策支援系統（advisers）可促進 CGM 的日常運用。
- 連網胰島素筆（smart pens）可進一步協助每日多次注射（MDI）治療並改善血糖結果。
- 以連續皮下胰島素輸注（CSII，胰島素幫浦）給藥可改善血糖、減少急性併發症並提升生活品質。
- 用於自動化胰島素輸注（automated insulin delivery, AID）的先進混合閉迴路（advanced hybrid closed-loop, AHCL）系統，目前是使用胰島素者最成功的治療模式。
- 針對不同使用族群、年齡層、生病日管理、規律運動以及住院與重症照護，皆需特別考量。
- 讓所有糖尿病患者更易取得糖尿病科技至關重要。
- 進一步的技術發展可望提升使用友善度、增加自動化程度，並改善長期使用者體驗與滿意度。

## Introduction / 前言

**EN:** While the development of insulin therapy vastly changed the course of diabetes management, technologic advances have allowed for more refined treatment methods. Diabetes technologies have classically been categorized as glucose monitoring devices and insulin delivery modalities. Capillary glucose measurements allowed for optimization of insulin doses, yet continuous glucose monitoring has provided exponential expansion in the frequency of glucose measures, permitting a more personalized approach. Insulin delivery has evolved from vials and syringes to insulin pens, connected insulin pens, and continuous subcutaneous insulin infusion (CSII) pumps. The latter forms the basis of more advanced technologies where insulin delivery is linked to sensor glucose readings.

**中文：** 胰島素治療的發展大幅改變了糖尿病管理的進程，而技術進步又使治療方法更為精細。糖尿病科技傳統上分為血糖監測裝置與胰島素輸注方式。微血管血糖測量讓胰島素劑量得以優化，但 CGM 將血糖測量頻率大幅（指數級）提升，使得對個人血糖動態的理解更佳、並提供更個人化的疾病管理機會。胰島素輸注則由瓶裝與針筒演進至胰島素筆、連網胰島素筆，以及 CSII 幫浦；後者構成更先進科技的基礎——胰島素輸注與感測器血糖讀值連動。

**EN:** Glycemic targets are no longer based solely on averages derived from glycated hemoglobin A1c (HbA1c); now the lived experience can be better approximated with continuous glucose data, including time spent in various glucose ranges.

**中文：** 血糖目標不再僅以糖化血色素（HbA1c）的平均值為依據；如今可藉由連續血糖資料（包含處於各血糖區間的時間）更貼近實際生活經驗，以引導照護。

## Understanding and Applying CGM Technologies in Diabetes / 理解並應用糖尿病的 CGM 技術

**EN:** Routine glucose monitoring is essential for people with diabetes to see how foods and exercise affect glucose, and to understand the impact of stress and illness. For those on insulin or insulinotropic therapy, frequent monitoring helps predict and reduce hypoglycemia risk, minimize glucose variability, and limit extended hyperglycemia with ketosis and ketoacidosis risk.

**中文：** 對糖尿病患者而言，例行血糖監測至關重要，可了解食物與運動如何影響血糖並造成血糖波動，亦能理解日常壓力與疾病對血糖的影響。對使用胰島素或促胰島素分泌（insulinotropic）治療者，頻繁監測有助於預測並降低低血糖風險、減少血糖變異，並限制伴隨酮症與酮酸中毒風險的長時間高血糖。

**EN:** The standard of care has been self-monitoring of blood glucose (SMBG) by fingerprick. Even a 7-point profile cannot fully capture glycemic profiles. Fingerprick lancing can be painful and suboptimal engagement is a barrier; most people with T1D or T2D do not achieve recommended SMBG frequency. Consequently, wearable CGM technologies are increasingly used, particularly for those on insulin.

**中文：** 過去照護標準為以指尖採血進行自我血糖監測（self-monitoring of blood glucose, SMBG）。即使每日多次（如餐前餐後與睡前共 7 點），仍無法完整呈現血糖樣態。指尖採血可能疼痛，且使用配合度不佳會成為達標障礙；多數 T1D 或 T2D 患者並未達到建議的 SMBG 頻率。因此，穿戴式 CGM 技術日益普及，尤以接受胰島素治療者為甚。

**EN:** Commercial CGM devices measure interstitial fluid glucose in the subcutaneous space, either via a transcutaneous filament or an implantable sensor. Readings are transmitted wirelessly every 1 to 5 minutes to a reader, smartphone/smartwatch app, or insulin pump (in AID systems). Devices transmitting data only when actively scanned are called intermittently scanned CGM (isCGM). Transcutaneous sensors last 7 to 14 days; implantable systems up to 180 days.

**中文：** 市售 CGM 裝置測量皮下組織間液（interstitial fluid）的葡萄糖，方式為經皮（transcutaneous）細絲穿刺植入或感測器本身植入（implantable）。讀值每 1 至 5 分鐘以無線方式傳送至讀取器、智慧型手機/手錶 app，或在 AID 系統中傳至胰島素幫浦。僅在使用者主動掃描時才傳輸資料者，稱為間歇掃描式 CGM（intermittently scanned CGM, isCGM）。經皮感測器配戴時間為 7 至 14 天；植入式系統可達 180 天。

**EN:** The first modern CGM devices (2000) provided 72 hours of retrospective data and required SMBG calibration; they were not approved for insulin dosing. Accuracy, utility, and size have improved dramatically. Several CGM devices are now approved for insulin-dosing decisions without a confirmatory SMBG—so-called nonadjunctive use.

**中文：** 首批現代 CGM 裝置（2000 年）提供 72 小時回溯資料，需以 SMBG 校正，且未獲准用於胰島素劑量決策。其準確度、效用與體積已大幅改善。現已有數款 CGM 裝置獲准在無需 SMBG 確認下做胰島素劑量決策——即所謂的非輔助性（nonadjunctive）使用。

### Sensor Calibration and Accuracy / 感測器校正與準確度

**EN:** Most CGM sensors are factory calibrated, eliminating user SMBG calibration; some require daily SMBG calibration, and a third method requires entering a calibration code before application. Calibration requirements may affect device choice and user engagement. Sensor accuracy can vary immediately after application (insertion-site trauma, reduced glucose bioavailability) and from pressure-induced attenuation (e.g., sleeping prone). Current systems have adapted, with no evidence of harm in adults; acute neonatal care must remain cognizant of these.

**中文：** 多數 CGM 感測器為原廠校正（factory calibrated），免去使用者以 SMBG 校正；部分需每日 SMBG 校正，第三種方式則需於貼附前輸入校正碼。校正需求可能影響裝置選擇與使用配合度。感測器準確度在剛貼附後可能因穿刺部位創傷與葡萄糖生物利用度降低而變動；亦可能因貼附部位受壓（如俯臥睡眠）而出現壓力誘發的訊號衰減（pressure-induced attenuation）。現行系統已因應這些情況，成人並無證據顯示有害；但急性新生兒照護仍須留意。

**EN:** Accuracy is assessed by comparison with concurrent reference blood glucose. The key metric is the mean absolute relative difference (MARD). Current CGM devices achieve MARD of 8% to 14%, comparing well with SMBG meters. Devices approved for nonadjunctive dosing or AID typically have MARD below 10%, although no prospective studies prove this is required.

**中文：** 準確度以同步參考血糖比對評估。關鍵指標為平均絕對相對差（mean absolute relative difference, MARD）。現行 CGM 裝置 MARD 介於 8% 至 14%，與 SMBG 血糖機相比表現良好。獲准非輔助性給藥或用於 AID 的裝置 MARD 通常低於 10%，惟尚無前瞻性研究證明此為必要條件。

**EN:** Because blood and interstitial fluid are different compartments, concordance must account for the time-lag for blood glucose to be reflected in interstitial fluid—on average 3 to 15 minutes, but higher when the rate of change exceeds 2.0 mg/dL/min (0.1 mmol/L/min). The consensus error grid evaluates the clinical significance of inaccuracies and assigns risk to errors by zone—the most important being a high CGM reading prompting insulin in the setting of physiologic hypoglycemia.

**中文：** 由於血液與組織間液為不同生理腔室、各具血糖動態，兩者一致性須考量血糖反映至組織間液所需的時間延遲（time-lag），平均約 3 至 15 分鐘；當血糖變化速率超過 2.0 mg/dL/min（0.1 mmol/L/min）時延遲更大。共識誤差網格（consensus error grid）評估感測器讀值誤差的臨床意義，依分區賦予不同風險——最關鍵者為：CGM 讀值偏高而實際為低血糖時導致施打胰島素，進而增加嚴重低血糖風險。

## CGM Technology Is Transformative for Diabetes Care / CGM 技術對糖尿病照護具變革性意義

**EN:** A fingerprick SMBG can measure glucose at any point, but is painful and hard to perform more than a few times daily. HbA1c, although a well-established marker for long-term complications, is not useful for day-to-day glucose. CGM provides objective information across all time periods of interest.

**中文：** 指尖 SMBG 可隨時測得血糖，但會疼痛且難以每日多次施行。HbA1c 雖為長期併發症的成熟指標，卻無法反映每日血糖。CGM 則能跨越糖尿病管理所關注的所有時段提供客觀資訊。

### The Limitations of SMBG and HbA1c / SMBG 與 HbA1c 的限制

**EN:** Fingerprick SMBG provides isolated snapshots, whereas CGM gives a complete view including overnight, peaks, and troughs. HbA1c reflects overall control over ~3 months; the UKPDS and DCCT showed improved HbA1c reduces retinopathy, nephropathy, neuropathy, and kidney failure. However, identical HbA1c results can mask very different patterns of hyper- and hypoglycemia. HbA1c is also influenced by nonglycemic factors (blood-cell turnover), so it may over- or under-estimate average glucose, creating risk of treatment overintensification and hypoglycemia in those whom HbA1c overestimates glucose.

**中文：** 指尖 SMBG 僅提供片段快照，而 CGM 提供完整全貌，含夜間、高峰與低谷。HbA1c 反映約 3 個月的整體控制；UKPDS 與 DCCT 證實改善 HbA1c 可降低視網膜病變、腎病變、神經病變與腎衰竭。然而，相同 HbA1c 可能掩蓋截然不同的高/低血糖樣態。HbA1c 亦受非血糖因素（紅血球更新）影響，故可能高估或低估平均血糖；對於 HbA1c 高估其血糖者，可能造成治療過度強化與低血糖風險。

## CGM Data Make It Possible to Understand and Manage Multiple Risk Factors / CGM 資料使理解與管理多重危險因子成為可能

**EN:** Beyond hyperglycemia, it is equally important to avoid hypoglycemia and limit glucose variability—the "triangle of diabetes care." A meta-analysis of five landmark studies (UKPDS, ACCORD, ADVANCE, VADT, PROactive) showed intensive HbA1c control has cardiovascular benefits but without improving overall mortality in T2D. The HbA1c–complications relationship is U- or J-shaped; lower is not always better. A 2018 Swedish study (33,915 individuals) found T1D with optimal HbA1c ≤6.9% (52 mmol/mol) still had a twofold risk of all-cause or cardiovascular death versus matched non-diabetic controls.

**中文：** 除高血糖外，避免低血糖與限制血糖變異同等重要——即「糖尿病照護三角」（triangle of diabetes care）。五項里程碑研究（UKPDS、ACCORD、ADVANCE、VADT、PROactive）的統合分析顯示，積極控制 HbA1c 有心血管益處，但未改善 T2D 整體死亡率。HbA1c 與併發症關係呈 U 型或 J 型曲線，並非越低越好。2018 年瑞典研究（33,915 人）發現，HbA1c 達理想 ≤6.9%（52 mmol/mol）的 T1D 患者，其全因或心血管死亡風險仍為配對非糖尿病者的兩倍。

### The Need to Manage Hypoglycemia / 管理低血糖的必要性

**EN:** Hypoglycemia from insulin and insulinotropic drugs causes cognitive impairment, confusion, irrational behavior, drowsiness, and may lead to seizures and coma, plus social/physical injury. Acute hypoglycemia may account for up to 10% of deaths in T1D under age 40. It is linked to cardiac dysrhythmia, inflammatory/endothelial dysfunction predisposing to atherosclerosis, and a prothrombotic coagulation state.

**中文：** 胰島素與促胰島素分泌藥物引起的低血糖會造成認知功能受損、意識混亂、不理性行為、嗜睡，並可能導致癲癇發作與昏迷，另有社交與身體傷害。急性低血糖可能占 40 歲以下 T1D 死亡的高達 10%。低血糖與心律不整、發炎反應與內皮細胞功能失調（促動脈粥樣硬化）以及凝血系統失衡（促血栓環境）相關。

### The Need to Manage Glycemic Variability / 管理血糖變異的必要性

**EN:** In both T1D and T2D, experimentally induced swings between hypo- and hyperglycemia cause greater endothelial dysfunction and oxidative stress than sustained hyperglycemia. In hospitalized patients, glycemic swings are associated with increased mortality and longer stays. CGM data show correlation between reactive oxygen metabolites and glucose variability in T2D.

**中文：** 在 T1D 與 T2D 中，實驗誘發的低/高血糖擺盪比持續高血糖造成更顯著的內皮功能失調與氧化壓力。住院患者的血糖擺盪與死亡率升高及住院延長相關。CGM 資料顯示 T2D 患者的活性氧代謝物（reactive oxygen metabolites）與血糖變異呈相關。

- Avoid hypoglycemia 避免低血糖

→ Better outcomes for people with diabetes 三者兼顧帶來更佳結果

## Making Daily Decisions Using CGM Technology / 運用 CGM 技術做每日決策

**EN:** The first major benefit is the impact on daily self-management. Seeing current glucose on demand makes users more aware of their status. More significantly, CGM provides trend arrow information on the direction and rate of change of glucose.

**中文：** CGM 的首要重大益處是對每日自我管理決策的影響。隨時可見即時血糖，使使用者更能掌握自身狀態。更重要的是，CGM 提供趨勢箭頭（trend arrow）資訊，呈現血糖的變化方向與速率。

### Trend Arrows and Projected Glucose / 趨勢箭頭與預測血糖

**EN:** Each system calculates direction and rate of change based on the previous 15 minutes of readings, displayed as a trend arrow. Combined with current glucose, users can decide on corrective action—taking insulin or carbohydrate—to prevent unwanted rise or fall, and time exercise, meals, and illness management.

**中文：** 各系統依前 15 分鐘讀值計算變化方向與速率，以趨勢箭頭顯示。配合當下血糖，使用者可決定是否採取矯正措施（施打胰島素或攝取碳水化合物），以防止血糖過度上升或下降，並安排運動、進食時機及生病期間的血糖管理。

### Making Insulin Dosing Decisions Incorporating Trend Arrows / 結合趨勢箭頭做胰島素劑量決策

**EN:** Several CGM devices are approved for insulin dosing without confirmatory SMBG. Recommendations must consider both arrow direction and rate of change; there is no standardization across devices. The "slide rule" approach evolved from JDRF/DirecNet advice (increase/decrease dose by 10% or 20%); patient-reported data revealed excessive self-adjustments (>100% increase, up to 50% decrease). More specific recommendations add/subtract a defined glucose value, but this burdens users with numeracy. Laffel et al and Aleppo et al published tables adding/subtracting fixed insulin amounts based on arrow and insulin sensitivity, recommended by the Endocrine Society. Ziegler et al and Bruttomesso et al added current glucose range as a variable. All methods are effective for falling glucose but tend to overestimate doses for rising glucose; no head-to-head clinical studies exist.

**中文：** 數款 CGM 裝置獲准在無需 SMBG 確認下做胰島素劑量決策。建議須同時考量箭頭方向與變化速率，且各裝置間無統一標準。「計算尺」（slide rule）方法源自 JDRF/DirecNet 建議（依趨勢將劑量增減 10% 或 20%）；患者自陳資料卻顯示自行調整過度（上升箭頭可使劑量增加超過 100%、下降箭頭可減少達 50%）。更具體的建議改以加減固定血糖值調整當下血糖，但增加使用者數學負擔。Laffel 等與 Aleppo 等發表依箭頭與胰島素敏感度（兒童與成人分表）加減固定胰島素量的表格，獲內分泌學會（Endocrine Society）推薦。Ziegler 等與 Bruttomesso 等再加入當下血糖區間作為變數。各法在血糖下降時皆有效，但對上升血糖傾向高估劑量；目前尚無頭對頭臨床比較研究。

## Using CGM-Derived Glucose Metrics in Clinical Practice / 臨床實務中運用 CGM 衍生指標

**EN:** CGM was initially centered on T1D but has widened to T2D and diabetes in pregnancy. A clinically essential component has been the adoption of internationally standardized metrics—chief among them, the time in ranges.

**中文：** CGM 起初聚焦於 T1D，後擴及 T2D 與妊娠糖尿病。其臨床關鍵在於採用國際標準化指標，其中以 time in ranges 最為重要。

### Time in Range in T1D and T2D / T1D 與 T2D 的 Time in Range

**EN:** Time in range (TIR) is the proportion of each day with glucose in three internationally defined ranges. For T1D/T2D, TIR target range is 70–180 mg/dL (3.9–10.0 mmol/L). A secondary metric, time in tight range (TITR), is 70–140 mg/dL (3.9–7.8 mmol/L), with a suggested target >70%, emerging for AID users and novel T2D agents.

**中文：** Time in range（TIR）指每日血糖落於三個國際共識區間的時間比例。對 T1D/T2D，TIR 目標範圍為 70–180 mg/dL（3.9–10.0 mmol/L）。次要指標 time in tight range（TITR）為 70–140 mg/dL（3.9–7.8 mmol/L），建議目標 >70%，正逐漸用於 AID 使用者與新型 T2D 藥物者。

**EN:** Time below range (TBR) is time below 70 mg/dL (3.9 mmol/L); time above range (TAR) is time above 180 mg/dL (10.0 mmol/L). TBR divides into level 1 (54–69 mg/dL, 3.0–3.9 mmol/L; alert threshold) and level 2 (<54 mg/dL, 3.0 mmol/L; clinically significant, may trigger counterregulation and cognitive impairment). Episodes count if lasting ≥15 minutes. TAR divides into 181–250 mg/dL (10.1–13.9 mmol/L) and >250 mg/dL (13.9 mmol/L); >250 mg/dL increases DKA risk in T1D.

**中文：** Time below range（TBR）為低於 70 mg/dL（3.9 mmol/L）的時間；time above range（TAR）為高於 180 mg/dL（10.0 mmol/L）的時間。TBR 分為第 1 級（54–69 mg/dL，3.0–3.9 mmol/L；警示閾值）與第 2 級（<54 mg/dL，3.0 mmol/L；具臨床意義，可能引發反調節反應與認知受損）。每次低血糖須持續 ≥15 分鐘方視為具臨床意義。TAR 分為 181–250 mg/dL（10.1–13.9 mmol/L）與 >250 mg/dL（13.9 mmol/L）；T1D 患者 >250 mg/dL 增加糖尿病酮酸中毒（DKA）風險。

- 高風險：因年齡、糖尿病病程、胰島素治療時間或低血糖感知障礙而高低血糖風險者。
- 妊娠數值證據有限；妊娠期 TIR 應與每日平均血糖併同考量，目標平均血糖 6.0–6.5 mmol/L。T2D 與 GD 妊娠尚無共識。GD = gestational diabetes mellitus（妊娠糖尿病）。

**EN:** TIR, TBR, and TAR are easily understood and immediately responsive to changes in medication, diet, and lifestyle. Evidence linking %TIR to complications is limited but increasing: in T2D, higher TIR is consistently associated with fewer macro- and microvascular complications; fewer T1D studies show lower TIR associated with increased microvascular/cardiovascular complications.

**中文：** TIR、TBR、TAR 易於理解，且能即時反映藥物、飲食與生活型態的改變。將 %TIR 連結至併發症的證據仍有限但日增：T2D 中較高 TIR 一致地與較少大血管/小血管併發症相關；較少的 T1D 研究顯示較低 TIR 與較多小血管/心血管併發症相關。

### Understanding Glycemic Variability Using CGM Data / 以 CGM 資料理解血糖變異

**EN:** Glucose variability is measured by the coefficient of variation (CV = SD/mean glucose). %CV correlates strongly with TBR; the recommended T1D target is ≤36%, above which hypoglycemia risk rises.

**中文：** 血糖變異以變異係數（coefficient of variation, CV = 標準差/平均血糖）衡量。%CV 與 TBR 高度相關；建議 T1D 目標為 ≤36%，超過此值低血糖風險上升。

## Time in Range in Elderly and High Hypoglycemia Risk / 老年與高低血糖風險族群的 Time in Range

**EN:** Older people are at higher risk for severe hypoglycemia due to age, diabetes duration, insulin duration, and hypoglycemia unawareness, exacerbated by cognitive/physical impairments and comorbidities. Risk increases with insulinotropic medications. Hypoglycemia in elderly T2D is associated with increased cardiovascular events, dementia, and death. Blinded CGM confirms frequent asymptomatic hypoglycemia. Avoiding hypoglycemia is suggested as a greater priority than minimizing HbA1c.

**中文：** 老年人因年齡、糖尿病病程、胰島素治療時間及低血糖感知障礙而嚴重低血糖風險較高，並因認知/身體障礙與共病而加劇；使用促胰島素分泌藥物時風險更增。老年 T2D 的低血糖與心血管事件、失智及死亡增加相關。盲性（blinded）CGM 證實無症狀低血糖頻繁發生。對老年糖尿病者，避免低血糖被建議為較最小化 HbA1c 更優先的目標。

**EN:** Consensus targets for high-risk/elderly emphasize reducing %TBR while preventing excessive hyperglycemia. Target range remains 70–180 mg/dL (3.9–10.0 mmol/L) but daily goal is TIR >50% (>12 h) rather than >70%, with %TBR <70 mg/dL kept below 1% (<15 min/day) and %TAR >250 mg/dL below 10% (<2 h 24 min/day).

**中文：** 高風險/老年族群的共識目標強調降低 %TBR、同時避免過度高血糖。目標範圍仍為 70–180 mg/dL，但每日目標改為 TIR >50%（>12 小時）而非 >70%，並將 <70 mg/dL 的 %TBR 控制於 <1%（每日 <15 分鐘）、>250 mg/dL 的 %TAR 控制於 <10%（每日 <2 小時 24 分鐘）。

**EN:** Multiple trials confirm benefit: the WISDM RCT reduced TBR <70 mg/dL by 48% at 26 weeks in T1D ≥60 years, sustained to 12 months. REPLACE showed a 56% TBR reduction in those ≥65 years. DIAMOND showed those ≥60 improved HbA1c and reduced variability similarly to younger adults. Bao et al showed T2D ≥65 on basal insulin improved TIR and reduced hypoglycemia. Feasibility was demonstrated up to age 91. TITR (70–140 mg/dL) is gaining attention with AHCL in T1D and potent novel T2D medications.

**中文：** 多項試驗證實益處：WISDM RCT 在 ≥60 歲 T1D 於 26 週使 <70 mg/dL 的 TBR 降低 48%，並維持至 12 個月。REPLACE 顯示 ≥65 歲者 TBR 降低 56%。DIAMOND 顯示 ≥60 歲者改善 HbA1c 並減少變異，效果與較年輕成人相當。Bao 等顯示使用基礎胰島素的 ≥65 歲 T2D 改善 TIR 並減少低血糖。已證實可用至 91 歲。隨 T1D 的 AHCL 與 T2D 強效新藥，TITR（70–140 mg/dL）日受重視。

## Time in Range During Pregnancy / 妊娠期間的 Time in Range

**EN:** During pregnancy, the goal is to safely increase %TIR quickly while reducing %TAR, %TBR, and variability. Consensus proposes >70% time at 63–140 mg/dL (3.5–7.8 mmol/L). Early in pregnancy women with pregestational T1D/T2D spend only ~50% (12 h/day) in target; this rises to 60% (T1D) and ~80% (T2D) by the third trimester. T1D women spend ~40% in hyperglycemia (TARp >140 mg/dL) at end of first trimester, falling to 33% by third trimester; T2D falls from 33% to 12%. T1D women spend more time (~4%, 1 h/day) below 70 mg/dL than T2D.

**中文：** 妊娠期間目標為安全且迅速提高 %TIR，同時降低 %TAR、%TBR 與變異。共識建議 >70% 時間落於 63–140 mg/dL（3.5–7.8 mmol/L）。妊娠早期，孕前即患 T1D/T2D 者僅約 50%（每日 12 小時）達標；至第三孕期，T1D 升至 60%、T2D 升至約 80%。T1D 婦女在第一孕期末約 40% 時間處於高血糖（妊娠期 TAR，TARp >140 mg/dL），第三孕期降至 33%；T2D 由 33% 降至 12%。T1D 婦女低於 70 mg/dL 的時間（約 4%，每日 1 小時）多於 T2D。

**EN:** The CONCEPTT trial (215 pregnant T1D women, target 63–140 mg/dL) confirmed TIRp rose ~10% from first to third trimester, and CGM improved %TIRp vs SMBG (68% vs 61%) and reduced %TARp (27% vs 32%) at 34–35 weeks without increased hypoglycemia. Observed %TBRp was 4% (1 h/day). A retrospective analysis of CONCEPTT and Swedish data (186 pregnancies) showed women who later delivered large-for-gestational-age (LGA) babies had significantly lower %TIRp from 6–8 weeks; a 7%–10% lower TIRp persisted until 34 weeks, with higher TARp. Increased glucose variability is an independent risk factor for LGA.

**中文：** CONCEPTT 試驗（215 名孕婦 T1D，目標 63–140 mg/dL）證實 TIRp 由第一至第三孕期上升約 10%，且 CGM 在 34–35 週使 %TIRp 較 SMBG 改善（68% vs 61%）並降低 %TARp（27% vs 32%），且未增加低血糖。觀察到的 %TBRp 為 4%（每日 1 小時）。對 CONCEPTT 與瑞典資料（186 例妊娠）的回溯分析顯示，日後產下大於胎齡兒（large-for-gestational-age, LGA）者，自孕 6–8 週起 %TIRp 即顯著較低；此 7%–10% 較低的 TIRp 持續至孕 34 週，並伴隨較高 TARp。血糖變異增加為產下 LGA 的獨立危險因子。

**EN:** Consensus recommends 63–140 mg/dL with %TIRp >70%, but data suggest this is met only in the final 3–4 weeks—too late for optimal neonatal outcomes. Women should aim for TIRp >70% and TARp (>140 mg/dL) <25% from as early as possible; even a 5% TIRp increase has clinically relevant neonatal benefits. TBRp targets: <4% below 63 mg/dL and <1% below 54 mg/dL. AHCL systems (AiDAPT trial) further facilitate these targets. T2D women spend one-third less time in hyperglycemia and can achieve up to 20% higher %TIRp. No firm targets for T2D/GD, but 63–140 mg/dL is recommended.

**中文：** 共識建議 63–140 mg/dL 且 %TIRp >70%，但資料顯示此目標僅在妊娠最後 3–4 週才達成——對最佳新生兒結果而言過遲。應自妊娠盡早起即力求 TIRp >70% 且 TARp（>140 mg/dL）<25%；即使 TIRp 僅增加 5% 亦有臨床相關的新生兒益處。TBRp 目標：<63 mg/dL 須 <4%、<54 mg/dL 須 <1%。AHCL 系統（AiDAPT 試驗）可進一步協助達標。T2D 婦女高血糖時間少三分之一，%TIRp 可高出達 20%。T2D/GD 尚無確定目標，但仍建議 63–140 mg/dL。

### Providing Support for Caregivers / 為照顧者提供支援

**EN:** Some apps allow users to invite family/caregivers to access glucose data remotely, including high/low alerts—invaluable for parents. Setting clear expectations on how the follower reacts to alerts is critical, especially during adolescence as teenagers seek autonomy.

### Mobile Health and Diabetes Smartphone Apps / 行動健康與糖尿病智慧手機 app

**EN:** Most CGM/AID users have smartphones. A November 2022 search found 85 diabetes mHealth apps (many from device companies, regulatory-assessed) plus 56 from independent providers (mostly blood-sugar diaries or meal planning). Adolescents and adults with T1D and T2D are receptive; an 80% of surveyed would share real-time data for tailored support. Regulatory oversight is a concern; the FDA lists registered apps, but mostly from hardware providers. The concept of "digiceuticals" (prescription-only validated apps) is emerging. The EASD/ADA Diabetes Technology Working Group recommends more oversight and offers calls to action for clinicians.

**中文：** 多數 CGM/AID 使用者擁有智慧手機。2022 年 11 月搜尋發現 85 個糖尿病 mHealth app（多來自裝置公司、經法規評估），另有 56 個來自獨立業者（多為血糖日誌或飲食規劃）。T1D 與 T2D 的青少年與成人接受度高；受訪者中 80% 願分享即時資料以獲量身支援。法規監督為一隱憂；FDA 列有註冊 app，但多為硬體業者所提供。「數位療法」（digiceuticals，僅憑處方取得且經臨床驗證的 app）概念正興起。EASD/ADA 糖尿病科技工作小組建議加強監督，並對臨床醫師提出行動呼籲。

### Box 36.1 — Definitions in Digital Health / 方塊 36.1 糖尿病數位健康定義

- **Telemedicine 遠距醫療**：以技術於遠距提供醫療，可為同步或非同步諮詢；涵蓋遠端監測、遠端專家會診、遠端協助、視訊看診、遠距諮詢與遠端教育。
- **Telemonitoring 遠端監測**：以資通訊技術監測、傳輸並分享地理上分隔個體間與病患健康狀態相關之資訊。以 CGM 監測血糖計量即為遠端監測之例。
- **mHealth 行動健康**：eHealth 的次領域，由行動裝置直接支援醫療與公共衛生實務。讀取並回報血糖感測器資料的智慧手機 app 即為其例。

## Ambulatory Glucose Profile (AGP) / Ambulatory Glucose Profile（AGP）

**EN:** The AGP is an accepted standard for summarizing dense CGM data, supporting shared decision making. Effective use is based on four components: (1) Data capture—14 consecutive days with ≥70% capture satisfactorily represents up to 90 days of data; (2) Time in ranges over the period, with consistent color-coding (green = desired, red = first priority, yellow/orange = high glucose needing adjustment); (3) Visual modal-day graphic; (4) Daily glucose profiles.

**中文：** AGP 是彙整密集 CGM 資料的公認標準，支援共享決策。有效運用奠基於四要素：(1) 資料擷取——連續 14 天且擷取率 ≥70%，即可充分代表最多 90 天的資料；(2) 該期間的 time in ranges，並採一致的色彩編碼（綠 = 理想、紅 = 首要處理、黃/橙 = 需調整的高血糖）；(3) 視覺化的「典型日」（modal day）圖；(4) 每日血糖曲線。

### Figure 36.9 — AGP Report (Example) / 圖 36.9 AGP 報告（範例）

**Time in Ranges（範例值）：** Very high >250 mg/dL: 20%；High 181–250 mg/dL: 24%（合併 >180: 44%）；Target 70–180 mg/dL: 46%（目標 >70%，每增 5% 具臨床效益）；Low 54–69 mg/dL: 5%；Very low <54 mg/dL: 5%（合併 <70: 10%）。

**Glucose Metrics 血糖計量指標：**

| Metric 指標 | Value 範例值 | Goal 目標 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Average glucose 平均血糖 | 175 mg/dL | <154 mg/dL |
| Glucose management indicator (GMI) | 7.5% | <7% |
| Glucose variability (%CV) 血糖變異 | 45.5% | ≤36% |

**EN:** The AGP modal-day graphic has four features: (1) target range (70–180 mg/dL) colored green; (2) the median line showing midpoint glucose; (3) the 25th–75th percentile band (IQR), showing daily trends and the influence of meals/medication, wider when more variable; (4) the 5th–95th percentile range (lightest band), indicating less frequent variability from behavior/lifestyle. The outer 5% of readings are outliers and not displayed.

**中文：** AGP 的典型日圖含四項要素：(1) 目標範圍（70–180 mg/dL）以綠色標示；(2) 中位數線顯示各時點血糖中點；(3) 第 25–75 百分位區帶（四分位距，interquartile range, IQR），呈現每日趨勢與餐食/藥物影響，越寬代表變異越大；(4) 第 5–95 百分位範圍（最淡區帶），反映源自行為/生活型態、較不頻繁的變異。最外 5% 讀值為離群值，不予顯示。

### Daily Glucose Profiles / 每日血糖曲線

**EN:** At the bottom of the AGP are individual daily traces for each of the 14 days, allowing interpretation of variability in the context of daily activities.

**中文：** AGP 報告最下方為 14 天各日的個別血糖軌跡，可結合每日活動脈絡解讀變異。

### Using the AGP Report Systematically / 系統化使用 AGP 報告

**EN:** The AGP can be used in a stepwise process. Step 5 uses the glucose management indicator (GMI)—a measure of glucose exposure over 14 days, calculated from mean glucose and reported in HbA1c units. If GMI is higher than or comparable to HbA1c, the person is a low/average "glycator." If GMI is noticeably lower than recent HbA1c, the person is a "high glycator," and intensification should use GMI, since basing it solely on HbA1c carries hypoglycemia risk. High red-cell turnover can falsely lower HbA1c.

**中文：** AGP 可採逐步流程使用。第 5 步使用血糖管理指標（glucose management indicator, GMI）——衡量 14 天血糖暴露，由平均血糖計算並以 HbA1c 單位呈現。若 GMI 高於或與 HbA1c 相近，該人屬低/平均「醣化者」（glycator）。若 GMI 明顯低於近期 HbA1c，則屬「高醣化者」，治療強化應依 GMI，因僅憑 HbA1c 強化有低血糖風險。紅血球更新率高可能使 HbA1c 假性偏低。

### Box 36.2 — Systematic Review of the AGP Report / 方塊 36.2 臨床實務中 AGP 報告的系統化判讀

- **Step 1 檢查資料擷取：** 確認連續 14 天擷取 >70% 資料，代表配合度良好。
- **Step 2 檢視 TBR：** 降低低血糖為核心。<70 mg/dL 的讀值若 <4% 即達標；若 ≥4%（尤其 ≥1% 低於 54 mg/dL）須探究原因。高風險者目標 <1%。
- **Step 3 檢視 TAR：** >180 mg/dL 的讀值若 <25% 即達標；若 ≥25%（尤其 ≥5% 高於 250 mg/dL）須探究。
- **Step 4 檢視血糖變異：** CV 目標 ≤36%；CV >36% 時找出 IQR 與第 5–95 百分位較寬的時段。
- **Step 5 比較 GMI 與近期 HbA1c：** 判定低/平均/高醣化者；高醣化者強化治療應僅依 GMI，以免單憑 HbA1c 造成低血糖。

**EN:** RCTs and real-world studies confirm CGM lowers HbA1c in children and adults with T1D (MDI or CSII) and adults with T2D (insulin or noninsulin), increases TIR, reduces TAR and TBR (including nocturnal), lowers variability, improves quality of life, and reduces hospital admissions for DKA and severe hypoglycemia.

**中文：** RCT 與真實世界研究證實，CGM 可降低 T1D 兒童與成人（MDI 或 CSII）及 T2D 成人（使用或不使用胰島素）的 HbA1c，提高 TIR，降低 TAR 與 TBR（含夜間），減少變異，改善生活品質，並降低因 DKA 與嚴重低血糖之住院。

### Improvements in Glycemic Outcomes in Type 1 Diabetes / T1D 血糖結果的改善

**EN:** A series of RCTs and observational studies unequivocally support CGM in T1D. The 2008 JDRF trial showed improved HbA1c and fewer severe hypoglycemia. RCTs showed HbA1c reduced up to 0.6% over 6 months. COMISAIR (3-year) showed 0.9% HbA1c reduction with CGM regardless of MDI or CSII—benefits of CGM exceed the insulin delivery mode. SELFY (children) showed 0.4% HbA1c reduction and 10% TIR improvement. Hypoglycemia reductions of 46%–70% were observed. IMPACT (isCGM) reduced hypoglycemia 38%, nocturnal 40%, very low (<45 mg/dL) 60%. RELIEF showed hospitalizations fell 49%, DKA 56%, comas 40% in 33,165 T1D.

**中文：** 一系列 RCT 與觀察研究明確支持 CGM 用於 T1D。2008 年 JDRF 試驗顯示改善 HbA1c 並減少嚴重低血糖。多項 RCT 顯示 6 個月內 HbA1c 最多降 0.6%。COMISAIR（3 年）顯示無論 MDI 或 CSII，CGM 皆使 HbA1c 降 0.9%——CGM 的益處超越胰島素輸注方式本身。SELFY（兒童）顯示 HbA1c 降 0.4%、TIR 升 10%。低血糖時間降幅達 46%–70%。IMPACT（isCGM）使低血糖降 38%、夜間 40%、極低血糖（<45 mg/dL）60%。RELIEF 顯示 33,165 名 T1D 住院降 49%、DKA 降 56%、昏迷降 40%。

## CGM Is Effective in Daily Management of T2D on Insulin Regimens / CGM 對使用胰島素方案的 T2D 有效

**EN:** T2D on intensive insulin benefit like T1D. DIAMOND (158 T2D on MDI) showed 0.3% HbA1c reduction; 20% more reached ≥1.0% reduction. A second RCT showed 0.85% vs 0.32% reduction (isCGM vs SMBG). REPLACE (224 T2D) showed 43% hypoglycemia reduction, 54% nocturnal, 64% level 2. RELIEF showed hospitalizations fell 39.4%, DKA 52.1%, hypoglycemia 10.8%, comas 31.9% in 40,846 T2D.

**中文：** 接受積極胰島素治療的 T2D 與 T1D 同樣受益。DIAMOND（158 名 MDI T2D）顯示 HbA1c 降 0.3%；多 20% 達 ≥1.0% 降幅。第二項 RCT 顯示 isCGM 降 0.85% vs SMBG 降 0.32%。REPLACE（224 名 T2D）顯示低血糖降 43%、夜間 54%、第 2 級 64%。RELIEF 顯示 40,846 名 T2D 住院降 39.4%、DKA 降 52.1%、低血糖降 10.8%、昏迷降 31.9%。

**EN:** For T2D on basal insulin, the MOBILE RCT (175 adults) showed CGM lowered HbA1c 0.4% and reduced time >250 mg/dL and hypoglycemia over 8 months. Retrospective studies show 0.8%–1.1% HbA1c reductions. RELIEF showed hospitalizations fell 63%, DKA 68%, hypoglycemia 58% in 5,933 basal-insulin T2D.

**中文：** 對使用基礎胰島素的 T2D，MOBILE RCT（175 名成人）顯示 CGM 於 8 個月內使 HbA1c 降 0.4%，並減少 >250 mg/dL 的時間與低血糖事件。回溯研究顯示 HbA1c 降 0.8%–1.1%。RELIEF 顯示 5,933 名使用基礎胰島素的 T2D 住院降 63%、DKA 降 68%、低血糖降 58%。

### Use of CGM in People With T2D Not on Insulin / 未使用胰島素之 T2D 的 CGM 應用

**EN:** Evidence is accumulating that non-insulin T2D can have clinically significant HbA1c reductions with CGM. A 12-week RCT (93 people) showed 0.29% reduction (isCGM vs SMBG, baseline 7.8%); a retrospective study (728 people) suggested larger reductions for those with higher baseline HbA1c. Variability is also reduced. CGM may reduce acute diabetes events.

**中文：** 證據漸增顯示未使用胰島素的 T2D 透過 CGM 亦可獲臨床顯著的 HbA1c 降幅。一項 12 週 RCT（93 人）顯示降 0.29%（isCGM vs SMBG，基線 7.8%）；一項回溯研究（728 人）顯示基線 HbA1c 越高者降幅越大。變異亦減少。CGM 或可減少需就醫的急性糖尿病事件。

## Telemonitoring and Telemedicine / 遠端監測與遠距醫療

**EN:** CGM enables remote consultations with shared glycemic data, and remote monitoring lets teams review AGP/TIR reports asynchronously. The "virtual diabetes clinic" has been a long-standing goal. Telemedicine improved HbA1c in T1D and T2D versus usual care; a meta-analysis (>3000 participants) found telemedicine at least as effective as usual care, especially in T2D. The VIDIKI pediatric study reduced HbA1c and parental burden. A 2020 meta-analysis (32 RCTs) in pregnancy showed improved HbA1c, fasting/postprandial glucose, and perinatal/postnatal outcomes.

**中文：** CGM 使遠距諮詢成為可能，醫病雙方共享血糖資料；遠端監測讓團隊可非同步檢視 AGP/TIR 報告。「虛擬糖尿病門診」長期為目標。遠距醫療相較常規照護改善 T1D 與 T2D 的 HbA1c；一項統合分析（>3000 人）發現遠距醫療至少與常規照護同等有效，尤以 T2D 為甚。VIDIKI 兒科研究降低 HbA1c 並減輕家長負擔。2020 年妊娠統合分析（32 項 RCT）顯示改善 HbA1c、空腹/餐後血糖及周產期/產後結果。

**EN:** The COVID-19 pandemic most thoroughly validated remote consultations. A review of 27 studies (69,294 T1D CGM users) found glycemia did not deteriorate in 25 of 27 cohorts and improved in 23; TIR increased in 19 cohorts (median 3.3%). 75% would continue remote appointments. Pediatric clinics were early adopters, with >80% of visits accomplished by telemedicine within 3 weeks.

**中文：** COVID-19 疫情最徹底驗證了遠距諮詢的價值。一項涵蓋 27 項研究（69,294 名 T1D CGM 使用者）的回顧發現，27 個世代中 25 個血糖未惡化、23 個改善；19 個世代 TIR 上升（中位數 3.3%）。75% 表示日後願續用遠距看診。兒科診所為早期採用者，3 週內 >80% 看診以遠距醫療完成。

### Telemonitoring Can Help Change Delivery of Care / 遠端監測有助改變照護提供方式

**EN:** Telemonitoring allows stratification of care—reviewing all active CGM users' profiles and triaging those needing consultation. People with problematic hypoglycemia, uncontrolled hyperglycemia, or high variability are prioritized; stable patients require less-frequent interaction. This focuses clinical value on those most in need.

**中文：** 遠端監測可分層照護——檢視所有使用 CGM 者的血糖樣態並分流需諮詢者。低血糖棘手、高血糖未控或變異高者優先；穩定者可較少互動。如此將臨床資源聚焦於最需要者。

### Digital Health Must Be Centered Around EMRs / 數位健康須以電子病歷為核心

**EN:** Opportunities of telemonitoring/telemedicine are only realized if CGM and pump data integrate into a unified EMR detailing health status, history, CGM metrics, and recent HbA1c. A proof-of-concept study showed shared inpatient-outpatient EMR improved follow-up. The iCoDE Project published CGM-EHR Integration Standards.

**中文：** 唯有將 CGM 與幫浦資料整合進統一電子病歷（electronic medical record, EMR，含健康狀態、病史、CGM 指標與近期 HbA1c），遠端監測/遠距醫療的潛力方能實現。一項概念驗證研究顯示共享住院–門診 EMR 改善追蹤。iCoDE 計畫已發表 CGM–EHR 整合標準。

## Insulin Delivery / 胰島素輸注

**EN:** In parallel with monitoring, insulin delivery technology has advanced rapidly, benefiting glycemic targets and quality of life.

**中文：** 與監測技術並行，胰島素輸注技術快速進步，有益於血糖達標與生活品質。

### Connected Insulin Pens / 連網胰島素筆

**EN:** "Smart" pens/caps pair with smartphone apps and can use CGM or fingerstick readings. They help users remember if/how much insulin was taken, preset dosing factors (correction factors, target glucose, insulin action duration, carbohydrate ratios), and offset calculation burden. They minimize insulin stacking, record timing/dose, and may remind for long-acting insulin and track temperature and remaining insulin. Observational studies show smart pens increase TIR by ~2 h/day in adults and children, with reduced hyper- and hypoglycemia, likely due to fewer omitted boluses. They are preferred over alternatives and may be cost beneficial.

**中文：** 「智慧」筆/筆帽與智慧手機 app 配對，可使用 CGM 或指尖血糖讀值。可協助使用者記得是否及注射多少胰島素，預設劑量因子（矯正因子、目標血糖、胰島素作用時間、碳水化合物比例），並減輕計算負擔。可降低胰島素疊加（stacking），記錄時間/劑量，並可提醒長效胰島素、追蹤溫度與餘量。觀察研究顯示智慧筆使成人與兒童 TIR 每日增加約 2 小時，並減少高/低血糖，可能因漏打 bolus 減少。智慧筆較其他方式更受偏好，且可能具成本效益。

**EN:** Many continue MDI as preferred. 2010–2018 registry data show increased pump use but 2018 prevalence varied 41%–73%. Smart pens offer those on MDI more precise therapy via dosing calculators and dose optimization based on history.

**中文：** 許多人仍偏好 MDI。2010–2018 年登記資料顯示幫浦使用增加，但 2018 年盛行率介於 41%–73%。智慧筆透過劑量計算器與依歷史優化劑量，為 MDI 使用者提供更精準的治療。

### Insulin Pumps / 胰島素幫浦

**EN:** Since the late 1970s, pump therapy has evolved considerably. Pumps deliver smaller doses (0.001–0.05 units), offer preset basal profiles (units/hr) varying through the day, and temporary basal rates for exercise (reduce) or illness (increase). Bolus calculators store insulin-to-carb ratios and correction factors. Insulin-on-board estimation prevents stacking. Infusion sets change every 3 days. Pumps form the backbone of more advanced technologies.

**中文：** 自 1970 年代後期起，幫浦治療大幅演進。幫浦可輸注更小劑量（0.001–0.05 單位），提供可隨時段變動的預設基礎輸注曲線（單位/小時），以及暫時基礎率（運動時調降、生病時調升）。Bolus 計算器儲存胰島素-碳水比與矯正因子。胰島素殘餘量（insulin-on-board）估算可防止疊加。輸注套件每 3 天更換。幫浦構成更先進科技的骨幹。

**EN:** Pump therapy improves glycemia, reduces hypoglycemia and DKA, and improves quality of life, with registries showing lower HbA1c. A Danish study found pump users had lower HbA1c but similar DKA (clinic-size dependent). A youth study found lower peripheral nerve abnormality and retinopathy despite similar HbA1c. Pump therapy benefits very young children (ISPAD recommends in preschoolers), older adults, recent diagnoses, and T2D. Those with highest baseline HbA1c derive the greatest benefit.

**中文：** 幫浦治療改善血糖、減少低血糖與 DKA、提升生活品質，登記資料顯示 HbA1c 較低。丹麥研究發現幫浦使用者 HbA1c 較低但 DKA 相近（與診所規模相關）。一項青少年研究發現即使 HbA1c 相近，幫浦組周邊神經異常與視網膜病變較少。幫浦治療對極幼兒（ISPAD 建議用於學齡前）、老年人、新診斷者及 T2D 皆有益。基線 HbA1c 最高者獲益最大。

### Additional Considerations With Pump Therapy / 幫浦治療的其他考量

**EN:** Pumps require an external device—a physical reminder problematic for some; intraperitoneal pumps are under investigation. Attrition is 4%–20%; severe hypoglycemia (adults) and being female/adolescent with depressive symptoms (youth) predict cessation. Adverse events relate to pump malfunction/infusion set failure; 15% of Teflon catheters kink on insertion. Counseling on DKA risk from occlusion/dislodgement is paramount, with sick-day plans and 24/7 access. Lipohypertrophy (20%–~50%) and lipoatrophy (2%–6%) are forms of lipodystrophy associated with glycemic variability; site rotation prevents them. Skin irritation/eczema is common (up to 89% of pump users report skin issues).

**中文：** 幫浦需外接裝置——對部分人是慢性病的具體提醒，造成困擾；腹腔內（intraperitoneal）幫浦仍在研究中。停用率為 4%–20%；成人嚴重低血糖、青少年女性且具憂鬱症狀者較易停用。不良事件多與幫浦故障/輸注套件失效相關；15% 的 Teflon 導管在置入時打折。須充分衛教因阻塞/移位導致 DKA 的風險，並備生病日計畫與 24/7 支援。脂肪增生（lipohypertrophy，20%–約 50%）與脂肪萎縮（lipoatrophy，2%–6%）屬脂肪失養（lipodystrophy），與血糖變異相關；輪替部位可預防。皮膚刺激/濕疹常見（高達 89% 幫浦使用者回報皮膚問題）。

## Sensor-Augmented Pump Therapy (SAP) / 感測器強化幫浦治療（SAP）

**EN:** SAP bridges insulin delivery with glucose data—sensor data viewable on a reader/phone or pump, but treatment decisions still fall to the user (e.g., a high-glucose alert requires a manual correction bolus). SAP provides the foundation for integrated systems. In 2008, the first RCT comparing SAP to pump therapy showed similar HbA1c reductions but less hypoglycemia in SAP; sensor use >60% led to greater HbA1c reduction. STAR-3 (SAP vs MDI+SMBG, 1 year) showed HbA1c reduction mediated by sensor wear, lowest in adolescents. Cost-effectiveness depends on persistent sensor use.

**中文：** SAP 將胰島素輸注與血糖資料連結——感測器資料可於讀取器/手機或幫浦上檢視，但治療決策仍由使用者負責（如高血糖警示仍須手動施打矯正 bolus）。SAP 為整合系統奠基。2008 年首項 SAP vs 幫浦治療 RCT 顯示 HbA1c 降幅相近但 SAP 低血糖較少；感測器使用 >60% 時 HbA1c 降幅更大。STAR-3（SAP vs MDI+SMBG，1 年）顯示 HbA1c 降幅取決於感測器配戴頻率，青少年最低。成本效益取決於持續使用感測器。

### Low Glucose Suspend / 低血糖暫停（Low Glucose Suspend）

**EN:** The next step used sensor data to affect delivery—suspending insulin is safer than ramping up. Low glucose suspend systems suspend basal insulin when sensor glucose reaches a predefined low threshold. The ASPIRE in-home study (T1D, 16–70 years) reduced hypoglycemic-range readings without HbA1c deterioration; glucose was stable 2 hours after nocturnal suspension. Another RCT in those with impaired hypoglycemia awareness reduced severe and moderate hypoglycemia (6 severe events in control vs 0 in suspend group). Fear of insulin interruption causing hyperglycemia/DKA was allayed by a study showing fasting glucose ~50 mg/dL higher but not clinically significant.

**中文：** 下一步是以感測器資料影響輸注——暫停胰島素比加大輸注更安全。低血糖暫停系統於感測器血糖達預設低閾值時暫停基礎胰島素。ASPIRE 居家研究（T1D，16–70 歲）減少低血糖範圍讀值且 HbA1c 未惡化；夜間暫停後 2 小時血糖穩定。另一項在低血糖感知障礙者的 RCT 減少了嚴重與中度低血糖（對照組 6 次嚴重事件 vs 暫停組 0）。對「暫停胰島素導致高血糖/DKA」的疑慮，已由一研究化解——空腹血糖約高 50 mg/dL 但無臨床意義。

### Predictive Low Glucose Suspend (PLGS) / 預測式低血糖暫停（PLGS）

**EN:** PLGS proactively interrupts insulin to prevent hypoglycemia—suspending when sensor glucose is predicted to be 20 mg/dL (1.1 mmol/L) above the low limit within 30 minutes, resuming after 30–120 minutes. Studies showed no increased morning ketosis vs SAP; younger children (4–9 yr) had more ketosis mornings, attributed to physiology rather than delivery method. The PROLOG RCT showed 31% reduction in time below 70 mg/dL vs SAP. A meta-analysis showed PLGS superior to SAP, with 8.8% lower overnight hypoglycemia risk. Carbohydrates needed to treat hypoglycemia decrease (median 9.9 g in one trial).

**中文：** PLGS 主動中斷胰島素以預防低血糖——當預測 30 分鐘內感測器血糖將高於低限 20 mg/dL（1.1 mmol/L）時即暫停，並於 30–120 分鐘後恢復。研究顯示晨間酮症未較 SAP 增加；幼童（4–9 歲）晨間酮症較多，歸因於生理而非輸注方式。PROLOG RCT 顯示相較 SAP，<70 mg/dL 時間減少 31%。統合分析顯示 PLGS 優於 SAP，夜間低血糖風險降低 8.8%。治療低血糖所需碳水化合物減少（某試驗中位數 9.9 公克）。

## Automated Insulin Delivery (AID) / 自動化胰島素輸注（AID）

**EN:** AID systems (closed loop, artificial pancreas) suspend insulin to mitigate hypoglycemia and ramp up to lessen hyperglycemia. Components: glucose sensor → algorithm → insulin pump. Algorithms include model predictive control (MPC, minimizes difference between predicted and target glucose), proportional integral derivative (PID, reactive—proportional/integral/derivative components), and fuzzy logic (imitates practitioner reasoning). Targets are treat-to-target (single) or treat-to-range (bounds). Early fully automated approaches caused postprandial excursions, leading to "hybrid" systems with user-input prandial insulin. Consensus suggests classifying delivery as user-initiated or algorithm-modulated.

**中文：** AID 系統（閉迴路 closed loop、人工胰臟 artificial pancreas）可暫停胰島素以減輕低血糖，並加大輸注以減少高血糖。組成：血糖感測器 → 演算法 → 胰島素幫浦。演算法包括模型預測控制（model predictive control, MPC，最小化預測與目標血糖差距）、比例-積分-微分（proportional integral derivative, PID，反應式——比例/積分/微分分量），與模糊邏輯（fuzzy logic，模仿醫師推理）。目標策略分治療至單一目標（treat-to-target）與治療至範圍（treat-to-range，上下界）。早期全自動方式造成餐後血糖飆升，因而採用使用者輸入餐食胰島素的「混合」（hybrid）系統。共識建議將輸注分類為使用者啟動（user-initiated）或演算法調控（algorithm-modulated）。

### Evidence Supporting AID / 支持 AID 的證據

**EN:** Data have grown exponentially; international consensus guidelines exist. From toddlers to geriatrics, AID uniformly increases %TIR (70–180 mg/dL) with reduced mean glucose, hyperglycemia time, and HbA1c, with no change or reduction in hypoglycemia. RCTs show TIR increases of 10%–15% (2.0–3.5 more hours/day) versus conventional pump, SAP, PLGS, or first-generation AID. A DCCT post hoc analysis shows each 10% lower TIR is associated with increased retinopathy (HR 64%) and microalbuminuria (HR 40%).

**中文：** 資料量呈指數成長；國際共識指引已制定。自幼兒至老年，AID 一致提高 %TIR（70–180 mg/dL），並降低平均血糖、高血糖時間與 HbA1c，低血糖則不變或減少。RCT 顯示相較傳統幫浦、SAP、PLGS 或第一代 AID，TIR 增加 10%–15%（每日多 2.0–3.5 小時）。DCCT 事後分析顯示 TIR 每降低 10%，視網膜病變進展（風險比 64%）與微量白蛋白尿（40%）風險增加。

**EN:** The first commercial AID (2017) had system-mandated exits (uncalibrated sensor, max insulin delivery, prolonged suspension), causing ~30% attrition and frequent alarms/sleep disruption. Newer factory-calibrated systems reduce exits and increase automation time. Real-world data (9451 users, 17% T2D) showed median 94% time in automation and TIR rising from 63.6% to 73.6% over 12 months, mirroring RCTs. Another analysis (4120 adopters) showed mean GMI 6.8% and 76% TIR after ~2 months; ≤15 yr and >15 yr groups both had 75% achieving >70% TIR.

**中文：** 首款市售 AID（2017）有系統強制退出（感測器未校正、達最大輸注、暫停過久），造成約 30% 停用及頻繁警示/睡眠中斷。較新的原廠校正系統減少退出並增加自動化時間。真實世界資料（9451 名使用者，17% 為 T2D）顯示自動化時間中位數 94%，12 個月內 TIR 由 63.6% 升至 73.6%，與 RCT 一致。另一分析（4120 名採用者）顯示約 2 個月後平均 GMI 6.8%、TIR 76%；≤15 歲與 >15 歲族群均有 75% 達 >70% TIR。

### Special Situations With AID Use / AID 使用的特殊情境

**EN:** Sick-day management requires checking ketones and changing infusion sets. With ketosis, it may be prudent to exit automation to open loop, give a correction dose, and use temporary basal rates (e.g., 200%); resume automation no sooner than insulin action duration (2–4 h). With steroids, remaining in automation depends on the system; some allow "boost" modes. Before minor procedures, AID increases the likelihood of acceptable glucose; temporary targets or exercise mode reduce insulin-on-board to mitigate hypoglycemia.

**中文：** 生病日管理須檢測酮體並更換輸注套件。出現酮症時，宜退出自動化轉為開迴路、給予矯正劑量並使用暫時基礎率（如 200%）；恢復自動化不應早於胰島素作用時間（2–4 小時）。使用類固醇時，是否續留自動化視系統而定；部分系統有「boost」模式。小手術前，AID 可提高血糖處於可接受範圍的機率；暫時目標或運動模式可降低胰島素殘餘量以減少低血糖。

### Insights From Open-Source AID Systems / 開源 AID 系統的啟示

**EN:** A do-it-yourself (DIY) community devised methods to remotely monitor glucose and alter delivery before commercial approval. Data (>550 users) showed DIY AID increased %TIR and reduced severe hypoglycemia. An RCT comparing open-source AID to CGM alone showed a 14% TIR difference (>3 h). Discontinuation due to connectivity occurred in ~5%. While caution is warranted (no regulatory approval), consensus guidelines say providers should support patients who choose DIY AID.

**中文：** 一個自製（do-it-yourself, DIY）社群在商業核准前即發展出遠端監測血糖並改變輸注的方法。資料（>550 名使用者）顯示 DIY AID 提高 %TIR 並減少嚴重低血糖。一項比較開源 AID 與單用 CGM 的 RCT 顯示 TIR 差距 14%（>3 小時）。約 5% 因連線問題停用。雖須謹慎（未獲法規核准），共識指引指出當病患選擇 DIY AID 時，醫療人員應予支持。

### Access to AID Systems / AID 系統的可近性

**EN:** Access has been hindered by selection bias toward "ideal" candidates (based on fingerstick frequency, visit attendance, HbA1c targets, diabetes duration), aligning with reported disparities. Yet those with highest baseline HbA1c/GMI have the greatest reductions. Clinicians should consider AID for all persons with diabetes, supported by consensus guidelines and aggregated RCT data (more automation increases %TIR; Fig. 36.11).

**中文：** 可近性曾因偏向「理想」候選者的選擇偏差而受阻（依指尖檢測頻率、回診次數、HbA1c 達標、糖尿病病程），與報告中的不平等相符。然而基線 HbA1c/GMI 最高者降幅最大。臨床醫師應對所有糖尿病者考量 AID，此有共識指引與彙整 RCT 資料支持（自動化越多 %TIR 越高；圖 36.11）。

## Anticipated Developments / 預期發展

**EN:** Despite AID's promise, daily management remains complex (meal announcements, sensor/set replacement, vigilance). Several strategies aim to mitigate meal-related excursions and ease use.

**中文：** 儘管 AID 前景看好，每日管理仍複雜（餐食輸入、感測器/套件更換、持續警覺）。多種策略旨在減輕餐後血糖飆升並簡化使用。

### Alternative Insulin Delivery Routes / 替代胰島素輸注途徑

**EN:** Inhaled insulin has a faster plasma peak (12–15 min) with return to baseline in <3 hours, improving postprandial excursion and reducing late hypoglycemia. Intraperitoneal delivery is more physiologic; a 24-hour trial (10 adults) showed increased time in 80–140 mg/dL (4.4–7.8 mmol/L) and lower mean glucose. Both reduce peripheral hyperinsulinemia (implicated in macrovascular risk), but remain nascent.

**中文：** 吸入式胰島素血漿尖峰較快（12–15 分鐘），<3 小時回到基線，改善餐後波動並減少晚發低血糖。腹腔內輸注較符合生理；一項 24 小時試驗（10 名成人）顯示 80–140 mg/dL（4.4–7.8 mmol/L）的時間增加且平均血糖較低。兩者皆可減少（與大血管風險相關的）周邊高胰島素血症，但仍處早期階段。

### Bihormonal and Adjunctive Therapies / 雙激素與輔助治療

**EN:** Bihormonal systems integrating insulin and glucagon mimic islet physiology and could enable fully automated systems without meal announcement; stable liquid glucagon formulations support development. Long-term effects of continuous glucagon infusion remain unknown. Adjunctive non-insulin therapies (pramlintide, GLP1-receptor agonists, SGLT inhibitors) blunt postmeal peaks and offer benefits beyond glycemia (lower insulin doses, weight stabilization/loss, cardiovascular potential).

**中文：** 整合胰島素與升糖素（glucagon）的雙激素系統可模擬胰島生理，並可能實現免餐食輸入的全自動系統；穩定的液態升糖素配方有助其發展。長期連續輸注升糖素的影響仍未知。輔助性非胰島素治療（pramlintide、GLP1 受體促效劑、SGLT 抑制劑）可削弱餐後尖峰，並提供血糖以外益處（降低胰島素劑量、穩定/減輕體重、潛在心血管效益）。

**EN:** Pramlintide (amylin analogue) delays gastric emptying and inhibits postmeal glucagon rise; it is approved as T1D adjunct but limited by subcutaneous administration. GLP1-receptor agonists promote glucose-responsive insulin secretion, suppress glucagon, and delay gastric emptying; one study showed lower postmeal peaks, 3.2 kg weight loss, and 26% lower total daily insulin, though limited by inability to promote insulin secretion in T1D. SGLT inhibitors lower the renal glucose threshold (glycosuria); benefits include lower insulin doses, weight loss, and cardiovascular outcomes in T2D, but T1D use is hindered by euglycemic ketoacidosis risk (ketosis signal—hyperglycemia—is absent). Not FDA-approved in T1D, but promising AID combination trials may change guidance.

**中文：** Pramlintide（amylin 類似物）延緩胃排空並抑制餐後升糖素上升；獲准為 T1D 輔助治療，但因需皮下注射而受限。GLP1 受體促效劑促進葡萄糖反應性胰島素分泌、抑制升糖素並延緩胃排空；一研究顯示餐後尖峰降低、體重減 3.2 公斤、每日總胰島素減 26%，惟受限於無法在 T1D 促進胰島素分泌。SGLT 抑制劑降低腎臟葡萄糖閾值（促糖尿）；益處含降低胰島素劑量、減重及 T2D 心血管結果，但 T1D 使用受阻於正常血糖酮酸中毒（euglycemic ketoacidosis）風險（偵測酮症的訊號——高血糖——不再出現）。在 T1D 尚未獲 FDA 核准，但有前景的 AID 合併試驗或將改變指引。

### Extended-Wear Infusion Set / 延長配戴輸注套件

**EN:** Standard infusion sets are worn ≤3 days to prevent inflammation, infection, pain, and site failure (→ hyperglycemia/DKA). In a pivotal trial (>22,000 wears), 7.4% were removed before 72 h with glucose >300 mg/dL and 3.3% for prolonged hyperglycemia. Extended-wear sets showed only 0.1% device-related hyperglycemia at 7 days, with 77% survival to 7 days and greater satisfaction, achieved via new fluid-path design preserving insulin stability. CE-mark and FDA approved.

**中文：** 標準輸注套件配戴 ≤3 天以防發炎、感染、疼痛與部位失效（→高血糖/DKA）。一項關鍵試驗（>22,000 次配戴）中，7.4% 在 72 小時前因血糖 >300 mg/dL 移除、3.3% 因持續高血糖移除。延長配戴套件在 7 天時僅 0.1% 裝置相關高血糖，77% 存活至 7 天且滿意度更高，係透過新流路設計維持胰島素穩定達成。已獲 CE 標誌與 FDA 核准。

### Tackling Exercise / 因應運動

**EN:** ≥150 min/week of moderate-to-vigorous aerobic exercise is recommended for adults; children ≥1 h/day. Exercise is burdensome due to relative hyperinsulinemia (suppressing counterregulation) and reliance on monitoring only glucose. A meta-analysis found AID during exercise improved TIR vs conventional pump. Most AID systems restrain insulin by raising target glucose, but must be set well in advance. Future work may use wearables and metabolites (lactate) for exercise detection. Bihormonal systems with continuous glucagon show promise.

**中文：** 成人建議每週 ≥150 分鐘中至高強度有氧運動；兒童每日 ≥1 小時。運動因相對高胰島素血症（抑制反調節）與僅監測血糖而具負擔。統合分析發現運動時 AID 較傳統幫浦改善 TIR。多數 AID 系統藉提高目標血糖限制胰島素，但須事先充分設定。未來或可用穿戴裝置與代謝物（乳酸）偵測運動。含連續升糖素的雙激素系統具潛力。

### Menstrual Cycles / 月經週期

**EN:** Insulin requirements fluctuate across the menstrual cycle due to estradiol/progesterone effects on insulin sensitivity, with increased hyperglycemia risk during periovulation and early luteal phases. CGM-based algorithms tracking insulin sensitivity could individualize treatment. A recent analysis of AID users found no difference in TIR or insulin across the cycle, suggesting technology may overcome menses-related variability.

**中文：** 因雌二醇/黃體素對胰島素敏感度的影響，胰島素需求隨月經週期波動，排卵前後與黃體早期高血糖風險增加。以 CGM 為基礎、追蹤胰島素敏感度的演算法可個別化治療。近期對 AID 使用者的分析發現週期間 TIR 或胰島素無差異，顯示科技或可克服月經相關變異。

## Use of Diabetes Technologies in Special Situations / 特殊情境下的糖尿病科技應用

### Technologies for Glycemic Control in Hospitalized Patients / 住院病人血糖控制的科技

**EN:** One in four noncritically ill hospitalized individuals has diabetes; an additional 25% experience hyperglycemia. Both diabetes and hyperglycemia are associated with prolonged hospitalization, complications, and mortality. CGM, pumps, AID, and bedside algorithms are receiving attention. However, hospitalization poses challenges due to highly variable insulin requirements (unsuppressed hepatic glucose production, reduced peripheral uptake, drug-induced resistance), nutritional changes, immobilization affecting sensor accuracy, and unfamiliarity with technologies.

**中文：** 每四名非重症住院者就有一名患糖尿病；另有 25% 出現高血糖。糖尿病與高血糖皆與住院延長、併發症與死亡相關。CGM、幫浦、AID 與床邊演算法日受重視。然而住院因胰島素需求高度變動（肝醣輸出未受抑、周邊攝取降低、藥物誘發阻抗）、營養改變、長期臥床影響感測器準確度及對科技不熟悉而具挑戰。

**EN:** The Endocrine Society suggests an operational target of 100–180 mg/dL (5.6–10.0 mmol/L) for noncritical patients. No agreement exists for neonates/pediatrics; a common preterm range is 72–144 mg/dL (4–8 mmol/L). Hyperglycemia increases morbidity/mortality, but tight control risks hypoglycemia (which augments ICU mortality). IV insulin burdens personnel, requires frequent glucose checks, and risks infection—especially concerning in NICU/PICU where minimizing blood draws is critical.

**中文：** 內分泌學會建議非重症病人操作目標 100–180 mg/dL（5.6–10.0 mmol/L）。新生兒/兒科尚無共識；常用的早產兒範圍為 72–144 mg/dL（4–8 mmol/L）。高血糖增加病態與死亡，但嚴格控制有低血糖風險（在加護環境會增加死亡）。靜脈胰島素增加人員負擔、需頻繁測血糖且有感染風險——在 NICU/PICU 尤須減少抽血，更顯重要。

### Diabetes Technologies in the NICU / 新生兒加護病房（NICU）的糖尿病科技

**EN:** After delivery, all infants have a transient glucose drop; preterm infants lack glycogen stores and have inadequate gluconeogenesis, raising hypoglycemia risk. Over 50% of premature neonates experience hypo- or hyperglycemia in the first days. CGM with dedicated algorithms is investigated to maintain target glucose. Neonatal hypoglycemia is associated with 20% lower literacy/proficiency scores; both hypo- and hyperglycemia may affect neurodevelopment. Older-generation devices have sufficient accuracy (MARD <14%) in preterm neonates. Two pivotal trials showed CGM-guided adjustment increased time in target ~10% with no hypoglycemia (<47 mg/dL, 2.6 mmol/L). Limitations include sensor artifacts from compression, medications (acetaminophen, vitamin C, alcohol, tetracycline), and lag time.

**中文：** 出生後所有嬰兒皆有短暫血糖下降；早產兒缺乏肝醣儲備且糖質新生不足，提高低血糖風險。逾 50% 早產新生兒在出生數日內出現低血糖或高血糖。研究以 CGM 搭配專屬演算法維持目標血糖。新生兒低血糖與識字/能力測驗分數低 20% 相關；高、低血糖皆可能影響神經發育。較舊世代裝置在早產兒準確度足夠（MARD <14%）。兩項關鍵試驗顯示 CGM 導引的調整使目標範圍時間增加約 10% 且無低血糖（<47 mg/dL, 2.6 mmol/L）。限制包括受壓、藥物（acetaminophen、維生素 C、酒精、tetracycline）造成的感測器假象與延遲時間。

### Figure 36.13 / 36.14 — Bedside Advisor & CGM Limitations / 圖 36.13、36.14 床邊顧問與 CGM 限制

- **Standard of care 標準照護：** 每日 3–4 次血糖機檢測 → 醫護人員 → 週期性調整葡萄糖與胰島素輸注。
- **CGM bedside advisor 床邊顧問：** CGM 感測器 24/7 資料 → 高/低血糖警示 → 床邊演算法（筆電）→ 醫護人員即時調整葡萄糖與胰島素輸注。
- **CGM 在早產兒的限制：** 時間延遲（血糖下降時正向誤差、上升時負向誤差）、漂移（drift，感測器表面腐蝕或生物膜）、壓迫假象（造成「假性」低血糖）。

### Diabetes Technologies in the PICU / 兒科加護病房（PICU）的糖尿病科技

**EN:** The SPECS trial used CGM to guide blood glucose sampling frequency in children after cardiac surgery; bedside glucose data fed an algorithm to guide IV insulin. It increased time in tight range (80–110 mg/dL, 4.4–6.1 mmol/L) without affecting healthcare-associated infections (primary outcome). Longitudinal analysis found those with moderate-to-severe hypoglycemia (<50 mg/dL and <40 mg/dL) had lower Bayley-III cognitive/motor scores. Glycemic targets in critically ill children remain controversial.

**中文：** SPECS 試驗以 CGM 導引心臟手術後兒童的血糖採樣頻率；床邊血糖資料供演算法導引靜脈胰島素。其增加緊密範圍（80–110 mg/dL, 4.4–6.1 mmol/L）的時間，但未影響醫療相關感染（主要結果）。縱貫分析發現出現中至重度低血糖（<50 mg/dL 與 <40 mg/dL）者，其 Bayley-III 認知/動作分數較低。重症兒童的血糖目標仍具爭議。

### Noncritical Hospitalized Patients / 非重症住院病人

**EN:** For adults with insulin-treated diabetes hospitalized for noncritical illness, real-time CGM with confirmatory point-of-care (POC) tests is recommended for insulin adjustments. CGM reduces time >180 mg/dL and increases detection of hypoglycemia <70 mg/dL. Account for factors affecting accuracy (medications, hypoperfusion). For pump users, ADA/AACE advocate maintaining the pump/CGM if the person can self-manage, has supplies, and personnel have expertise; otherwise transition to basal-bolus MDI. For pregnant women on a pump admitted for labor, maintain pump therapy.

**中文：** 對因非重症住院、接受胰島素治療的成人，建議使用即時 CGM 並以床邊即時檢測（point-of-care, POC）確認後調整胰島素。CGM 減少 >180 mg/dL 的時間並增加 <70 mg/dL 低血糖的偵測。須留意影響準確度的因素（藥物、灌流不足）。對幫浦使用者，ADA/AACE 主張若病人能自我管理、備有耗材且人員具專業，應維持幫浦/CGM；否則轉為基礎-餐前（basal-bolus）MDI。對使用幫浦待產的孕婦，建議維持幫浦治療。

### AID in People Who Are Hospitalized / 住院者的 AID

**EN:** An RCT in noncritical patients with hyperglycemia on enteral/parenteral nutrition showed AID with faster-acting insulin aspart increased time in 100–180 mg/dL (5.5–10.0 mmol/L) by >30% vs conventional treatment (<50% had preadmission diabetes); hypoglycemia time was not significantly reduced. In noncritical T2D, fully automated AID without meal announcements safely increased time in target by ~25% (6 more hours/day) without increasing total daily insulin, without disrupting ward workflow.

**中文：** 一項針對接受腸道/腸外營養之高血糖非重症病人的 RCT 顯示，使用速效 insulin aspart 的 AID 使 100–180 mg/dL（5.5–10.0 mmol/L）的時間較常規治療增加 >30%（<50% 入院前有糖尿病診斷）；低血糖時間未顯著減少。在非重症 T2D，免餐食輸入的全自動 AID 安全地使目標範圍時間增加約 25%（每日多 6 小時），未增加每日總胰島素，且未擾亂病房流程。

### AID in Pregnancy / 妊娠的 AID

**EN:** Women with diabetes aim for near-normal targets (63–140 mg/dL, 3.5–7.8 mmol/L) in pregnancy; for T1D the goal is >70% TIRp, <4% below 63 mg/dL, and <1% below 54 mg/dL; T2D/GD targets remain undetermined. AID has been tested and proven safe. A 4-week randomized crossover study showed AID increased time in tight range (63–140 mg/dL) by ~15%, achieving >70% overnight vs SAP, maintained through a 14-week extension. An ongoing multicenter trial tests AID in T1D with HbA1c 6.5%–10%.

**中文：** 妊娠期糖尿病婦女力求接近正常的目標（63–140 mg/dL, 3.5–7.8 mmol/L）；T1D 目標為 TIRp >70%、<63 mg/dL <4%、<54 mg/dL <1%；T2D/GD 目標仍待確定。AID 已受測試並證實安全。一項 4 週隨機交叉研究顯示 AID 使緊密範圍（63–140 mg/dL）時間增加約 15%，夜間達 >70%（優於 SAP），並於 14 週延伸期維持。一項進行中的多中心試驗測試 AID 用於 HbA1c 6.5%–10% 的 T1D。

## Future Directions / 未來方向

**EN:** Digital technologies have transformed diabetes management, reducing complications and burden—most notably the widespread introduction of AID in T1D, effectively replacing pancreatic glucose homeostasis. The goal is to make AID the standard of care in T1D and to help all women with diabetes on insulin plan successful pregnancies. A parallel objective is wearable CGM as standard of care in T2D, including non-insulin users and pregestational T2D in pregnancy, with smart pens for insulin-treated T2D.

**中文：** 數位科技已改造糖尿病管理，減少併發症與負擔——最顯著者為 AID 在 T1D 的廣泛導入，有效取代胰臟的血糖恆定功能。目標是使 AID 成為 T1D 的照護標準，並協助所有使用胰島素的糖尿病婦女規劃成功妊娠。並行目標為使穿戴式 CGM 成為 T2D 的照護標準（含未使用胰島素者及妊娠期孕前 T2D），並對接受胰島素治療的 T2D 使用智慧筆。

**EN:** CGM-derived metrics will be used as study outcomes in medication trials, enabling agencies (FDA, EMA) to add them to drug labeling. Further developments are expected for delivery devices, sensors, and AI algorithms—including noninvasive sensing and long-term implantable sensors measuring glucose, ketones, and other parameters over years. New medications/formulations will be developed for use with technology. The ultimate goal is a fully inclusive EMR reflecting complete health status with up-to-date glucometric profiles, enabling AI-based therapy adjustment. This great opportunity requires a paradigm shift in attitudes, education, technology, and service design.

**中文：** CGM 衍生指標將作為藥物試驗的研究終點，使法規機構（FDA、EMA）得以將其納入藥物標示。輸注裝置、感測器與 AI 演算法可望持續進展——包括非侵入式感測與可長期植入、能同時測量葡萄糖、酮體與其他參數達數年的感測器。將為搭配科技使用而開發新藥物/配方。最終目標為一套涵蓋性的 EMR，反映完整健康狀態與最新血糖計量資料，使以演算法為基礎的 AI 治療調整成為可能。此重大機會須在態度、教育、技術與服務設計上進行典範轉移。
