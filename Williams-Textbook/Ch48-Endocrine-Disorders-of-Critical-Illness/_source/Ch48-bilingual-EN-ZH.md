## Key Points / 重點摘要

**EN:**
- In critical illness, characteristic endocrine alterations develop; these alterations differ in the acute versus prolonged phase of illness and complicate the diagnosis of preexisting endocrine disease.
- Acute critical illness is characterized by low levels of thyroid hormone and insulin-like growth factor 1, and high levels of cortisol, which is presumably adaptive and mainly brought about by peripheral alterations in hormone metabolism.
- In patients with prolonged critical illness, a central suppression of the somatotropic, thyrotropic, and adrenocorticotropic axes progressively develops, which may impair rehabilitation, although treatment indications and regimens remain to be studied in adequately powered trials.
- Safe blood glucose control in critically ill patients requires a validated protocol, with accurate and frequent blood glucose measurements and avoidance of insulin boluses.
- The ideal blood glucose target in critically ill patients not receiving early parenteral nutrition remains unclear.
- Critically ill patients have low vitamin D concentrations, which is probably adaptive in the acute phase of illness.

**中文：**
- 在重症疾病中，會產生特徵性的內分泌變異；這些變異在急性期與延長重症期（prolonged phase）有所不同，並增加了既有內分泌疾病診斷的複雜性。
- 急性重症的特徵為低濃度甲狀腺素與生長因子-1（IGF1），以及高濃度皮質醇（cortisol）；這推測屬於適應性反應（adaptive response），主要由周邊激素代謝變異所驅動。
- 在延長重症期的患者中，生長激素軸、甲狀腺素軸與腎上腺皮質素軸漸進性出現中央抑制（central suppression），這可能損害復健與復原，然而治療適應症與方案仍有待充份統計力的臨床試驗研究。
- 重症患者的安全血糖控制需要經過驗證的處方方案，配合精確且頻繁的血糖測量，並避免胰島素靜脈衝擊給藥（bolus injection）。
- 在未接受早期腸外營養（early parenteral nutrition）的重症患者中，最佳血糖控制目標目前仍未明確。
- 重症患者常出現低維生素 D 濃度，在急性期這可能亦屬於適應性反應。

## 1. Introduction / 引言

**EN:**
As a consequence of severe medical illness, major surgery, or trauma, critically ill patients need vital organ monitoring or support to prevent or treat life-threatening insults, in order to maintain or restore homeostasis. As part of the stress response, profound endocrine alterations occur, including neuroendocrine changes, insulin resistance and stress hyperglycemia, and alterations in vitamin D, which have been assumed to be adaptive. However, in patients who remain dependent on intensive care for a prolonged period of time, a different endocrine phenotype develops, which may be maladaptive. The illness-induced endocrine alterations often complicate the diagnosis of preexisting endocrine disease and may have important therapeutic implications. This chapter provides a review of the most prominent endocrine changes during acute and prolonged critical illness, as well as the diagnostic and potential clinical implications.

**中文：**
由於嚴重內科疾病、重大手術或創傷，重症患者需要生命器官監測或支持，以預防或治療致命性打擊並維持或恢復體內恆定。作為壓力反應的一部份，患者會出現深刻的內分泌變異，包括神經內分泌改變、胰島素抗性與壓力性高血糖，以及維生素 D 的改變，傳統上認為這些改變屬於適應性（adaptive）。然而，在長期依賴加護病房（ICU）的患者中，會發展出不同的內分泌表型，這可能是適應不良的（maladaptive）。疾病引起的內分泌變異常使既有內分泌疾病的診斷變得極其困難，並具有重要的治療含義。本章綜述急性與延長重症期中最顯著的內分泌變化及其診斷與潛在臨床意義。

## 2. Alterations in the Somatotropic Axis / 生長激素軸的變異

**EN:**
In the acute phase of critical illness, growth hormone (GH) secretion increases, with increased pulse frequency and amplitude and elevated trough concentrations. Similarly, peripheral growth hormone resistance occurs, with lowered expression of hepatic GH receptors, leading to low concentrations of insulin-like growth factor 1 (IGF1), IGF-binding protein 3 (IGFBP3), and acid-labile subunit (ALS). Low levels of binding proteins also lead to increased elimination of IGF1, enhancing low IGF1 bioavailability. High GH concentrations directly induce lipolysis and gluconeogenesis, hence increasing substrate to vital tissues for survival, while indirect and energy-consuming anabolic effects of GH—mediated by IGF1—are postponed. Systemic inflammation is likely a main driver of these changes, although the concomitant fasting response may also play a role.

**中文：**
在急性重症期，生長激素（GH）分泌增加，表現為脈衝頻率與振幅增加，以及最低基礎濃度升高。同時，周邊發生生長激素抗性（GH resistance），肝臟 GH 受體表達下降，導致第 1 型類胰島素生長因子（IGF1）、IGF 結合蛋白質 3（IGFBP3）及酸不穩定亞基（ALS）濃度降低。結合蛋白濃度低下亦加速了 IGF1 的清除，進一步降低 IGF1 的生物利用度。高濃度的 GH 直接促進脂解作用（lipolysis）與糖質新生（gluconeogenesis），從而為維持生命的關鍵組織提供能量基質；而由 IGF1 介導的間接且耗能的合成代謝效果（anabolic effects）則被暫緩。全身性發炎反應可能是這些改變的主要驅動力，儘管伴隨的禁食反應也可能發揮作用。

**EN:**
In patients developing prolonged critical illness, IGF1 concentrations remain low, predominantly explained by a lack of hypothalamic stimulation, since GH pulse amplitude and trough concentrations decline while peripheral GH responsiveness at least partially recovers. Administration of the ghrelin-like GH-releasing peptide 2 (GHRP2), and even more the combination of GHRP2 and GH-releasing hormone (GHRH), restore the pulsatility of GH secretion with a proportional increase in IGF1, its binding proteins IGFBP3, and ALS. When coadministered with thyrotropin-releasing hormone (TRH), GHRP2 infusion also decreases markers of catabolism in prolonged critically ill patients, suggesting that central hyposomatotropism and hypothyroidism may contribute to the wasting phenotype of such patients.

**中文：**
在進入延長重症期的患者中，IGF1 濃度持續保持低檔，這主要是由於下視丘刺激不足所致，因為 GH 脈衝振幅與基礎濃度均下降，而周邊 GH 反應性則至少部份恢復。給予類似 ghrelin 的 GH 釋放肽 2（GHRP2），特別是 GHRP2 聯合 GH 釋放激素（GHRH），可恢復 GH 分泌的脈衝性，並使 IGF1、IGFBP3 及 ALS 成比例增加。當 GHRP2 與甲狀腺素釋放激素（TRH）聯合給藥時，還能降低延長重症患者的異化代謝標記（markers of catabolism），提示中央型生長激素低下與甲狀腺素低下可能是導致此類患者消耗表型（wasting phenotype）的原因之一。

## 3. Alterations in the Thyroid Axis / 甲狀腺軸的變異 (NTIS)

**EN:**
In acute critical illness, levels of the active form of thyroid hormone, triiodothyronine (T3), rapidly decline, explained by altered peripheral metabolism. Mechanistically, the activity of type 1 deiodinase is attenuated while type 3 deiodinase activity increases, leading to increased peripheral conversion of thyroxine (T4) secreted by the thyroid gland to the biologically inactive reverse T3 (rT3). Simultaneously, degradation of T3 is increased, altogether leading to low T3 levels and a decreased T3:rT3 ratio. Concomitantly, concentrations of the pituitary hormone thyrotropin (TSH) and of T4 briefly rise, followed by rapid normalization of TSH and T4. These alterations have been designated as euthyroid sick syndrome, nonthyroidal illness syndrome (NTIS), or acute low T3 syndrome.

**中文：**
在急性重症期，活性甲狀腺素三碘甲狀腺原氨酸（T3）濃度迅速下降，這是由周邊代謝改變所致。在機轉上，第 1 型去碘酶（D1）活性受抑制，而第 3 型去碘酶（D3）活性增加，導致甲狀腺分泌的四碘甲狀腺原氨酸（T4）在周邊加速轉化為無生物活性的逆向 T3（rT3）。同時，T3 的降解增加，最終導致低 T3 濃度及 T3:rT3 比值下降。與此同時，腦下垂體促甲狀腺素（TSH）及 T4 濃度短暫上升，隨後迅速恢復正常。這些改變被稱為正常甲狀腺功能病態症候群（euthyroid sick syndrome）、非甲狀腺疾病症候群（nonthyroidal illness syndrome, NTIS）或急性低 T3 症候群。

**EN:**
In prolonged critical illness, alterations in peripheral metabolism often persist: T3 concentrations further decline due to the virtually absent pulsatile TSH secretion, and this results in low TSH and T4 concentrations. The low pituitary hormone secretion is explained by hyposecretion of thyrotropin-releasing hormone (TRH). An autopsy study revealed low TRH gene expression in patients dying after prolonged critical illness compared with sudden death. Interventional studies supplementing thyroid hormone in the acute phase of critical illness have not shown benefit on outcomes.

**中文：**
在延長重症期中，周邊代謝改變通常持續存在：由於脈衝式 TSH 分泌幾乎完全消失，T3 濃度進一步下降，並導致低 TSH 與低 T4 濃度。腦下垂體激素分泌低下是由下視丘促甲狀腺素釋放激素（TRH）分泌不足所致。解剖研究顯示，長期重症死亡患者的 TRH 基因表達顯著低於猝死對照組。在急性重症期補充甲狀腺素的干預性研究並未顯示對預後有益。

## 4. Alterations in the Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal Axis / 下視丘-腦下垂體-腎上腺軸的變異 (CIRCI)

**EN:**
Critically ill patients undergo profound changes in the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis. Following acute stress, adrenocorticotropic hormone (ACTH) briefly rises, accompanied by elevated cortisol concentrations. Thereafter, ACTH concentrations rapidly decline to low levels, while cortisol concentrations remain persistently elevated. The persistent hypercortisolemia in the presence of suppressed ACTH (ACTH-cortisol dissociation) after the hyperacute phase is explained by suppressed breakdown of cortisol. Moreover, less cortisol binding to the binding proteins cortisol-binding globulin (CBG) and albumin due to lower levels and CBG binding affinity will increase the active free cortisol fraction.

**中文：**
重症患者的下視丘-腦下垂體-腎上腺（HPA）軸發生深刻改變。在急性壓力發生後，促腎上腺皮質素（ACTH）短暫上升，伴隨皮質醇（cortisol）濃度升高。此後，ACTH 濃度迅速降至低檔，而皮質醇濃度則持續保持高檔。在超急性期過後，ACTH 受抑制但皮質醇持續偏高（ACTH-皮質醇游離解離現象，ACTH-cortisol dissociation），主要原因是皮質醇在周邊的分解被顯著抑制。此外，由於皮質醇結合球蛋白（CBG）及白蛋白濃度下降且 CBG 結合親和力降低，游離活性皮質醇（free cortisol）的比例顯著增加。

**EN:**
In prolonged critically ill patients, central suppression of the HPA axis develops while cortisol breakdown remains reduced. The central suppression of the HPA axis is illustrated by the progressive decline of total and free cortisol concentrations to even normal concentrations after 4 weeks in the ICU in the absence of elevated ACTH, despite persisting illness. Adrenal glands from patients dying after prolonged critical illness are characterized by a reduced expression of ACTH-regulated genes and a severely disturbed adrenocortical structure.

**中文：**
在延長重症期患者中，HPA 軸出現中央型抑制，而皮質醇分解依然減少。HPA 軸中央抑制的特徵在於：儘管疾病持續存在，在加護病房待滿 4 週後，總皮質醇與游離皮質醇濃度漸進性下降至正常範圍，且無 ACTH 補償性升高。長期重症死亡患者的腎上腺組織切片顯示，ACTH 調控基因的表達顯著減少，且腎上腺皮質結構發生嚴重紊亂（disturbed adrenocortical structure）。

## 5. Insulin Resistance and Stress Hyperglycemia / 胰島素抗性與壓力性高血糖

**EN:**
In response to severe inflammation and elevations in counter-regulatory hormones, critically ill patients usually develop hyperglycemia, which has been associated with poor outcomes. However, the ideal glucose target remains unclear. While early single-center RCTs (Leuven trials) demonstrated mortality and morbidity benefits from tight glycemic control (target 80–110 mg/dL for adults) using intensive insulin therapy, the subsequent multicenter NICE-SUGAR trial showed increased mortality and severe hypoglycemia risk with tight glucose control compared to a blood glucose target of < 180 mg/dL. Key differences between these landmark trials included target blood glucose in the control group, accuracy of monitoring, and parenteral feeding strategies.

**中文：**
因應嚴重的發炎反應與升糖激素（counter-regulatory hormones）的升高，重症患者常發生高血糖，這與不良臨床預後相關。然而，最佳血糖控制目標至今仍未完全統一。雖然早期單中心 RCT（Leuven 試驗）顯示利用積極胰島素治療將血糖嚴格控制在正常範圍（成人 80–110 mg/dL）可降低死亡率與併發症，但後續大型多中心 NICE-SUGAR 試驗卻顯示，與維持血糖 < 180 mg/dL 相比，嚴格血糖控制增加了死亡率與嚴重低血糖風險。這些指標性試驗之間的主要差異包括對照組的血糖目標、血糖監測的精確度以及早期腸外營養（parenteral nutrition）的施用策略。

## 6. Alterations in Vitamin D / 維生素 D 的變異

**EN:**
Critically ill patients frequently exhibit low concentrations of 25-hydroxyvitamin D [25(OH)D]. In the acute phase, low vitamin D levels may represent an adaptive response or acute phase reactant change (due to hemodilution, decreased binding proteins, and increased tissue uptake). Randomized controlled trials evaluating high-dose vitamin D supplementation in unselected ICU patients have not consistently demonstrated clinical benefit in terms of hospital length of stay or mortality, although benefit in severely deficient subgroups remains an area of ongoing study.

**中文：**
重症患者普遍出現 25-羥基維生素 D [25(OH)D] 濃度低下。在急性期，低維生素 D 濃度可能代表一種適應性反應或急性期反應物改變（源於血液稀釋、結合蛋白減少與組織攝取增加）。在未經篩選的重症患者中評估高劑量維生素 D 補充的隨機對照試驗未能在住院天數或死亡率方面一致顯示臨床獲益，儘管在極度缺乏的次族群中是否獲益仍持續受到研究。
